First Measurement Of The Radiative Process Negative Kaon Proton Going To Lambda Gamma At Beam Momenta 520-750 Mev/c Using The Crystal Ball Detector
This dissertation presents the first measurement of <italic>K</italic><super> &minus;</super> photoproduction from the unstable &Lambda; hyperon using the inverse reaction <italic>K<super>&minus;</super>p</italic> &rarr; &Lambda;<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math>. The experiment was conducted in the C6 beamline of the Alternating Gradient Synchrotron at the Brookhaven National Laboratory using the Crystal Ball detector, which is a highly segmented multi-photon spectrometer covering nearly 4&pi; steradian. Data were collected at eight beam momenta: 520, 558, 580, 629, 657, 690, 720, and 750 MeV/<italic>c</italic>, corresponding to center-of-mass energies of 1571 to 1676 MeV. The dominant event type in the data is <italic>K<super>&minus;</super>p</italic> &rarr; <italic>neutral</italic> &rarr; <math> <f> <g>g</g><sup>&prime;</sup></f> </math><italic>s</italic> + <italic>n</italic>. The kinematic-fit technique is used to reconstruct the <italic>K<super>&minus;</super>p</italic> &rarr; &Lambda;<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> &rarr; <italic>n</italic>&pi;<super>0</super> + <math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> &rarr; <italic>n</italic> + 3<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> events. The two primary background reactions, <italic>K<super>&minus; </super>p</italic> &rarr; &Lambda;&pi;<super>0</super> and <math> <f> K<sup>- </sup>p&rarr;K<sup>0</sup><inf>S</inf>n&rarr;<g>p</g><sup> 0</sup><g>p</g><sup>0</sup>+n</f> </math>, are determined simultaneously using the 4 photon events. The differential cross sections for both reactions are in good agreement with the existing data. The reaction <italic>K<super>&minus;</super>p</italic> &rarr; &Sigma;<super> 0</super>&pi;<super>0</super> &rarr; &Lambda;<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> + &pi;<super>0</super> &rarr; <italic>n</italic> + 5<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> is measured using the 5-photon events. It is used to determine the momenta of the <italic>K</italic><super>&minus;</super> beams. The <italic> K<super>&minus;</super>p</italic> &rarr; &Lambda;<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> candidates in the 3-photon event sample are corrected for the background from interactions in the target walls and the two primary background reactions. In the case of the &lsquo;3<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> + <italic>n</italic>&rsquo; events, we found that the background from the empty target runs is negligible. The total background subtraction from the &Lambda;<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> sample is about 70&ndash;80% for both types of event. The cross section for <italic>K<super>&minus;</super>p</italic> &rarr; &Lambda;<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> is limited to the angular interval cos &thetas;* = &minus;0.8 to 0.8. It is obtained as the combination of the results from the 3- photon and &lsquo;3<math> <f> <g>g</g></f> </math> + <italic>n</italic>&rsquo; events. Due to the smallness of the radiative cross section, our results have substantial uncertainties. They are approximately constant for our eight data sets with <math> <f> <g>s</g><inf>K<sup>- </sup>p&rarr;<g>L</g><g>g</g></inf></f> </math> (&minus;0.8 &lt; cos &thetas;* &lt; 0.8) &ap; 25&ndash;45 &mu;<italic> b</italic>. The angular distributions are smooth and do not show a characteristic feature which would have enabled the determination of the individual contributions of various hyperon radiative decays.
